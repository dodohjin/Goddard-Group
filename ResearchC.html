  • C1-1-2. Activation mechanism class A

    #1518. "The mechanism for ligand activation of the GPCR–G protein complex".

    We established the G-Protein (GP) first mechanism for G-Protein Coupled Receptors (GPCRs) in which the unactivated G-Protein with GDP attached binds first inactive GPCR to form a precoupled GPCR-GP complex with intracellular region partially coupled but not activated. Then binding of agonist causes the Ga part of the GP to open releasing GDP for GP signaling. It is this precoupled structure that is relevant to drug design, but there are not yet any crystal structures (all current structures are for the fully activated agonist-GPCR-GP structure after activation and release of the GDP.

    Binding of a ligand to this precoupled structure can lead to full activation (full agonist), to partial activation (partial agonist), or to no activation (inverse agonist) depending on where the ligand binds to the precoupled GPCR-GP complex.
