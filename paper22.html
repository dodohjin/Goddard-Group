1518. The mechanism for ligand activation of the GPCR–G protein complex.

A. Mafi, S.-K. Kim and W.A. Goddard III. Proc. Natl. Acad. Sci. U.S.A. 119 (18):Art. No. e2110085119 (2022) DOI: 10.1073/pnas.2110085119

Topics: Mechanism activation of class A GPCRs.


We show, for 15 Class A GPCRs for 6 different G-Proteins, that in all cases the inactive GPCR (Σ0) is precoupled (Σ2, not activated) after binding to the G-Protein (S1). Σ2 remains unactivated until the agonist (say morphine) binds to the precoupled state (Σ3’) causing the G-Protein to open (Σ4’), releasing the GDP for signaling (relieve pain). Depending on the agonist binding site in Σ3’, the agonist may be a full agonist (small activation barrier (ΔG*) with large negative ΔG), a partial agonist (larger activation barrier (ΔG*) with less large negative ΔG), or an inverse agonist (positive ΔG). This makes the structure of Σ3’ the most important for drug design, Only Σ0 and Σ4’ have been seen by xray or cryoEM.
